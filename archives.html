Émile d’Aragon: The Apothecary of Time

Born in 1887 in Avignon, Émile d’Aragon was a quiet observer of botanical life and its effects upon the skin. He lived privately, studying the regenerative capacities of plants in a time when dermatology barely existed. He believed skin was not simply tissue, but memory — that every blemish held a biography and every recovery a rhythm. In 1919, he began documenting skin formulas in what would become the legendary Compendium d’Aragon.

His theories were decades ahead. He postulated that cellular signaling through scent and touch could stimulate regenerative behavior — foreshadowing discoveries in neurocosmetics and biomimetic peptides. Despite ridicule, he persisted in his remote lab in Aix-en-Provence until his sudden passing in 1929.

His writings, believed lost, were unearthed in 2025 and now form the scientific and poetic basis of Époque Labs’ skincare philosophy.

Glossary of Terms

Actif régénératif: A regenerative active compound designed to stimulate skin renewal.

Compendium: A carefully curated collection of Émile’s formula notes, treatments, and philosophies.

Derme sonore: “Sonic skin” — Émile’s belief that skin responds to sound and rhythm.

La Méthode Émile™: The four-phase ritual of Époque: Purify, Signal, Revive, Shield.

Suspension florale: A floral infusion designed to stabilize active botanicals.
